Thinking about what to wear to a milonga can be stressful. Should you dress very elegant? Should it be comfortable? What about shoes? In this guide, you'll find everything you need to know about milonga attire.

<GamzeQuote>
Clothing at a milonga is a way to express yourself. Feel comfortable, look beautiful, and dance!
</GamzeQuote>

## General Rules

### Milonga Dress Code

| Milonga Type | Expectation |
|--------------|-------------|
| Traditional | Elegant, classic |
| Modern | Smart-casual |
| Practica | Casual, comfortable |
| Special night/festival | Very elegant |

### Basic Principles

1. **Freedom of movement** - You must be able to dance
2. **Cleanliness** - Hygiene is very important
3. **Comfort** - You must be able to stand for hours
4. **Elegance** - A milonga is a special evening

---

## For Women

### Dress and Skirt Selection

**Ideal Skirt/Dress:**
- Below knee or midi length
- Opens when turning (A-line, circle skirt)
- Allows freedom of movement
- Body-hugging but not restricting

**Things to Avoid:**
- Too long (gets tangled in feet)
- Too short (sitting problem)
- Too tight (restricts movement)
- Too slippery fabric

<Callout type="tip" title="Skirt Test">
Do a few pivots and ochos in your skirt at home. Is it comfortable? Does it open? Does it tangle in your feet?
</Callout>

### Upper Garments

**Good Options:**
- Blouse
- Wrap top
- Tank top (covered shoulders preferred)
- Knit top (be careful on warm nights)

**Things to Consider:**
- Not too low-cut
- Partner's hand won't slip
- Sweat-absorbing fabric

### Can Pants Be Worn?

Yes! But be careful:
- Prefer wide-leg or palazzo
- Tight pants restrict leg movement
- Pair with an elegant blouse

### Women's Tango Shoes

#### Heel Height

| Height | Who It Suits |
|--------|--------------|
| 5-6 cm | Beginners |
| 7-8 cm | Intermediate |
| 9+ cm | Advanced |

#### Shoe Types

1. **Closed Toe:** More stable, for beginners
2. **Open Toe:** Elegant, for advanced
3. **Sandals:** For summer, experienced dancers
4. **Practice Shoes:** Low heel, comfortable

#### Sole Types

- **Suede sole:** Ideal for dancing, slides well
- **Leather sole:** Medium slide
- **Rubber sole:** ‚ùå Not suitable for dancing

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
Don't step on the floor with street shoes! It dirties the floor and you can't slide.
</Callout>

### Accessories

**Recommended:**
- Small earrings
- Delicate necklace
- Bracelet (won't catch on anything)

**Things to Avoid:**
- Large, dangling earrings
- Long necklaces (catches on partner)
- Large rings (digs into hand)
- Purse (leave at table)

## For Men

### Shirt Selection

**Ideal:**
- Long sleeve shirt (can roll up sleeves)
- Cotton, breathable fabric
- Solid color or subtle pattern
- Slim fit (not too loose)

**Color Recommendations:**
- White (classic)
- Light blue
- Black
- Burgundy, navy

<Callout type="tip" title="Extra Shirt">
Keep a spare shirt in your bag. A sweaty shirt is uncomfortable for both you and your partner.
</Callout>

### Pants Selection

**Ideal:**
- Dress pants
- Chinos
- Dark jeans (smart-looking)

**Things to Avoid:**
- Sweatpants
- Shorts
- Ripped jeans
- Too tight (restricts movement)

### Men's Tango Shoes

**Options:**
1. **Tango shoes:** Custom-made, ideal
2. **Leather shoes:** Flat or very low heel
3. **Loafers:** Leather-soled
4. **Dance sneakers:** For practicas

**Note:**
- Leather or suede sole
- Comfortable and flexible
- Clean and well-maintained

### Accessories

**Optional:**
- Thin belt
- Cufflinks
- Tie (rarely needed)

**Unnecessary:**
- Large watch
- Wallet (creates bulge in pocket)
- Keychain

---

## For Both Genders

### Hygiene Is Very Important!

Close dancing at milongas:
- **Shower** - Before going
- **Deodorant** - Plenty
- **Mints/gum** - For breath
- **Perfume** - Light (not too much!)
- **Spare clothes** - If you sweat

### By Season

**Summer:**
- Light, breathable fabrics
- Bring extra shirt/top
- Stay hydrated

**Winter:**
- Layers (remove when dancing)
- Bring a bag for coat
- Change shoes after arriving

---

## Conclusion

Your milonga outfit should make you feel confident and comfortable. Remember: you're there to dance, not to pose. Choose clothes that allow movement, shoes that let you glide, and accessories that don't interfere. Most importantly, be yourself!
