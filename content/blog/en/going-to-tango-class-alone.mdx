Tango is a "two-person" dance, but the tango journey starts "individually". Let me explain why you should go to a tango course even if you don't have a partner (or even especially if you don't).

## 1. We Do "Partner Rotation" in Classes üîÑ

The golden rule of tango education: **Everyone dances with everyone.**

Here is how I set up the system in my lessons: While practicing a move, every 3-5 minutes I say "Come on, changing partners!".

This has 2 enormous benefits for you:
1.  **You Learn Better:** If you only get used to your own partner, you also memorize their mistakes. Dancing with different people teaches "real" tango, that is, leading and following.
2.  **No One Waits in the Corner:** The fear of "Oh, I came alone, will I be a wallflower?" does not exist with us. Everyone is part of the dance circle.

Tango course is the most enjoyable way to break this chain. Here, doctors, engineers, students, retirees are side by side. Our common ground is dance.
If you don't have a partner, this is a wonderful environment to build **new friendships** (and yes, sometimes new loves ü•∞).

Coming without a partner is actually an investment you make in yourself. You take a step according to your own schedule, for your own happiness, without having to say "let's go" to anyone. This self-confidence reflects on your dance too.
