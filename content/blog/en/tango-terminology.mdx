Tango has its own language - a mix of Spanish and unique terms developed in the milongas of Buenos Aires. Understanding these terms will help you follow lessons, communicate with partners, and feel more connected to the culture.

<GamzeQuote>
When you learn the words, you learn the culture. Tango is not just steps - it's a whole world with its own language.
</GamzeQuote>

## Basic Terms

### The Embrace

<TangoTerm
  term="Abrazo"
  pronunciation="ah-BRA-zo"
  definition="The embrace. The way partners hold each other in tango. Can be open (abierto) or close (cerrado)."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Conexión"
  pronunciation="ko-nek-SYON"
  definition="Connection. The physical and emotional bond between partners."
/>

### Movement

<TangoTerm
  term="Caminata"
  pronunciation="ka-mi-NA-ta"
  definition="The walk. The most fundamental movement in tango."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Paso"
  pronunciation="PA-so"
  definition="Step. A single step in any direction."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Cruce"
  pronunciation="KRU-se"
  definition="Cross. When one foot crosses in front of or behind the other."
/>

## Roles

<TangoTerm
  term="Líder"
  pronunciation="LI-der"
  definition="Leader. The person who initiates and guides the movement."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Seguidor/a"
  pronunciation="se-gi-DOR/a"
  definition="Follower. The person who responds to and interprets the lead."
/>

<Callout type="info" title="Modern Note">
Traditionally, men led and women followed. Today, both roles are open to everyone. Many dancers learn both!
</Callout>

## Directions & Positions

<TangoTerm
  term="Adelante"
  pronunciation="a-de-LAN-te"
  definition="Forward. Moving toward your partner."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Atrás"
  pronunciation="a-TRAS"
  definition="Backward. Moving away from your partner."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Costado"
  pronunciation="kos-TA-do"
  definition="Side. Moving laterally."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Paralelo"
  pronunciation="pa-ra-LE-lo"
  definition="Parallel system. Partners' feet mirror each other (both on same foot)."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Cruzado"
  pronunciation="kru-SA-do"
  definition="Cross system. Partners are on opposite feet."
/>

## Key Figures

<TangoTerm
  term="Ocho"
  pronunciation="O-cho"
  definition="Eight. A figure-eight pattern made with the feet, either forward or backward."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Giro"
  pronunciation="HI-ro"
  definition="Turn. A rotation around a central axis."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Molinete"
  pronunciation="mo-li-NE-te"
  definition="The follower's pattern around the leader during a giro: forward, side, back, side."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Sacada"
  pronunciation="sa-KA-da"
  definition="Displacement. When one partner's leg appears to displace the other's."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Barrida"
  pronunciation="ba-RRI-da"
  definition="Sweep. Pushing your partner's foot with yours along the floor."
/>

## Adornments

<TangoTerm
  term="Adornos"
  pronunciation="a-DOR-nos"
  definition="Embellishments. Decorative movements added to the basic steps."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Boleo"
  pronunciation="bo-LE-o"
  definition="A whip-like leg movement, usually led but can be an adorno."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Gancho"
  pronunciation="GAN-cho"
  definition="Hook. When one partner hooks their leg around the other's."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Lápiz"
  pronunciation="LA-pis"
  definition="Pencil. Drawing circles or lines on the floor with the toe."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Golpe"
  pronunciation="GOL-pe"
  definition="Tap. A rhythmic tap of the foot on the floor."
/>

## Social Dance Terms

<TangoTerm
  term="Milonga"
  pronunciation="mi-LON-ga"
  definition="1. A tango social dance event. 2. A type of faster, rhythmic tango music."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Tanda"
  pronunciation="TAN-da"
  definition="A set of 3-4 songs played together, usually by the same orchestra."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Cortina"
  pronunciation="kor-TI-na"
  definition="Curtain. Short music played between tandas signaling partner change."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Cabeceo"
  pronunciation="ka-be-SE-o"
  definition="The traditional way of asking for a dance using eye contact and a nod."
/>

<TangoTerm
  term="Ronda"
  pronunciation="RON-da"
  definition="The counter-clockwise flow of dancers around the floor."
/>

<Callout type="tip" title="Milonga Etiquette">
Understanding these social terms is essential before attending your first milonga. They're the unwritten rules!
</Callout>

## Music Terms

<TangoTerm

/>

<TangoTerm

/>

<TangoTerm

  pronunciation="or-KES-ta"

## Useful Phrases

| Spanish | English | When to Use |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| ¿Bailamos? | Shall we dance? | Inviting someone |
| Gracias | Thank you | After each tanda |
| Con permiso | Excuse me | Moving through crowd |
| Buenas noches | Good evening | Greeting at milonga |
