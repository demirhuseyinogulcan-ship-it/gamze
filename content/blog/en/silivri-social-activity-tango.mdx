Silivri is one of the most beautiful escape points of Istanbul. Its sea, calmness, summer vibes... But when winter comes and summer residents leave, or when the work-home routine sets in, one sometimes asks: *"So, what is there to do in Silivri?"*

Sitting in cafes and walking on the beach are fine for a while. If you, like me, are looking for a different activity that will "add color to my life, improve me, and allow me to meet new people", my answer is clear: **Tango.**

Why? Let me explain.

## 1. A New Social Circle in Silivri

Tango is the most social dance in the world. When you start a course, you don't just learn dance steps; you join a brand new "tribe".

Who attends our classes in Silivri? Doctors, teachers, engineers, students, housewives... The common language of people from different professions and different ages becomes **dance**.

*   Chats before the lesson,
*   Coffees drunk together after the lesson,
*   "Practice Hours" we organize (dancing together events)...

Suddenly, you realize you have made not just classmates, but close friends.

## 2. "Unwinding" Therapy for White-Collar Workers

If you are one of those commuting from Silivri to Istanbul for work, I know very well what that traffic and work stress mean. When you return home in the evening, your mind is full, your shoulders are tense.

Tango is the most elegant way to relieve this stress.
When you enter the class and that first music starts, work emails, unpaid bills, or traffic ordeal fly away from your mind. Because tango requires focus. Focus on your partner, listen to the music, take your step... Only **the now** exists at that moment. We call this "dance meditation". üßò‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è

## 3. The Most Fun Way to Do Sports

If you say "I need to start sports but I get bored walking on the treadmill in the gym", tango is just for you.

*   It corrects your posture.
*   It improves balance and coordination.
*   You burn calories without realizing it (we sweat quite a bit in an hour lesson!).
